How ACCESS Works

ACCESS is a roleplaying strategy game where players build characters with intersecting identities, then navigate real-world scenarios that grant or restrict access to resources. Grounded in Kimberl√© Crenshaw's intersectionality framework and Tara Yosso's Community Cultural Wealth model, the game reveals how overlapping identities create compounding barriers ‚Äî and how communities develop unique assets to resist and navigate those systems.

Two Frameworks, One Game

Crenshaw shows us where the barriers are ‚Äî how intersecting identities create unique positions of disadvantage that single-axis frameworks miss. Yosso shows us what communities bring ‚Äî six forms of Cultural Wealth developed through, not despite, navigating those barriers. Both are essential to understanding inequality fully.

Community Cultural Wealth Cards

At the start of each round, players receive Cultural Wealth Cards based on their intersectional identity. Characters with more marginalized identities receive more cards ‚Äî because Yosso argues these capitals are developed through the experience of navigating hostile systems. These cards can be played during the Reveal phase to shift outcomes. A character with deep Resistant Capital might outperform a privileged character who has no community wealth to draw on.

Round Structure (~10 min)

  • Phase 1 ‚Äî Read (1 min): A life scenario is revealed.
  • Phase 2 ‚Äî Community Wealth Draw: Cards are drawn based on identity scores.
  • Phase 3 ‚Äî Discuss (3 min): How will identities and cultural wealth interact?
  • Phase 4 ‚Äî Choose Approach (1 min): Select a strategy for navigating the scenario.
  • Phase 5 ‚Äî Reveal & Play Cards (3 min): Outcomes are revealed. Players may play a Wealth Card to shift results and narrate why.
  • Phase 6 ‚Äî Reflect (2 min): Debrief connects to Crenshaw and Yosso.
INSTRUCTOR NOTE

When students play a Cultural Wealth Card, ask them to narrate it: "Describe how your character's Navigational Capital shows up in this moment. What specifically do they know, who do they call, what do they do?" The card is a prompt for storytelling, not just a mechanical bonus. This is where the richest learning happens.
